# Analysis of Cardiac Physiology

This project analyzes cardiac hemodynamics using the CircAdapt simulation tool. It focuses on pressure, volume, and flow relationships in the heart under normal conditions and in the presence of aortic valve stenosis.

## 📌 Objective

* Study the mechanical function of the heart using simulation
* Analyze pressure-volume relationships in the cardiac cycle
* Understand valve dynamics and blood flow patterns
* Investigate the effects of aortic valve stenosis

---

## 🧠 Background

The heart functions as a pump, generating pressure to circulate blood.
Key physiological variables include:

* Pressure in heart chambers and vessels
* Volume changes during the cardiac cycle
* Blood flow through valves

These relationships are commonly visualized using:

* **Wiggers diagram** (pressure vs time)
* **Pressure-Volume (PV) loops**

---

## ⚙️ Simulation Tool

* **Software:** CircAdapt Simulator
* Models the cardiovascular system using lumped-parameter modelling
* Allows adjustment of physiological and pathological conditions

---

## 🧪 Simulation Studies

### 🟢 Normal Sinus Rhythm

#### Pressure-Volume Loop

The cardiac cycle consists of four phases:

* Filling
* Isovolumetric contraction
* Ejection
* Isovolumetric relaxation

#### Valve Behavior

* **Mitral valve:** Opens when atrial pressure > ventricular pressure
* **Aortic valve:** Opens when ventricular pressure > aortic pressure

#### Observations

* Smooth and periodic cardiac cycle
* Normal pressure and flow patterns
* Efficient pumping function

---

### 🔴 Aortic Valve Stenosis

Aortic stenosis is a condition where the aortic valve opening is reduced, restricting blood flow.

#### Effects:

* Increased resistance to blood ejection
* Higher ventricular pressure required
* Reduced cardiac efficiency

#### Key Relation

The pressure drop across the valve is estimated using:

[
\Delta P \approx 4v^2
]

#### Observations

* Increased pressure in the left ventricle
* Reduced outflow through the aortic valve
* Enlarged PV loop area (increased cardiac work)

---

## 📊 Key Observations

* PV loop area represents cardiac work
* Stenosis significantly increases workload on the heart
* Pressure differences across valves indicate flow resistance
* Valve dysfunction alters normal cardiac dynamics

---




## 🎯 Conclusion

The simulation demonstrates how cardiac performance depends on valve function and pressure relationships.
Aortic stenosis increases cardiac workload and disrupts normal blood flow, highlighting the importance of valve integrity in cardiovascular health.
